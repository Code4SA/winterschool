Want to learn more about Data Journalism?

Code for South Africa
Winter School

August 03 - 05, 2015

Followed by half a day a week, for three weeks

What's this about?

The Winter School aims to train working journalists in the fundamentals of data-driven storytelling. We will also offer training in social media as a journalism tool, with a focus on the discovery and verification of content. Participants will be introduced to all aspects of data and social media journalism, with structured course material.

This will be coupled with hands-on practical work to ensure that you are able to translate what you’ve learned into great stories. After completion, participants will “hit the ground running” to be able to incorporate their new skills in their day-to-day work as journalists. Another outcome of the course is that participants will produce their own data-driven multimedia story that can be taken back to their workplace or, in the case of freelancers, offer it for sale and add to their own portfolios.

It’s data JOURNALISM, not DATA journalism

You don’t need to be a coder or have technical skills to practice data journalism. What you do need are basic Excel skills and a nose for a good story to help you explore the data and find the hidden stories.

When?

August 03 - 05 for an intensive three day bootcamp, followed by three half-day sessions over the following three weeks. Participants will be required to commit to the full course duration and will receive certificates of completion at the end of the course.

What do we cover?

Data Proficiency

Basic gathering, collating and verification of data.

Data Viz and Storytelling

Uncover the stories hidden in the data and how best to tell and visualise your story.

The Journalist's Toolkit

Free tools that will help you to be a better journalist.

Social Media Journalism

How to use social media as a journalism tool, with a focus on verification of social media content and free tools.

What does it cost?

The cost per participant is R5000, however full and partial sponsorships are available to participants who are able to provide motivation for this assistance.

Who should apply?

Freelance and full-time journalists interested in adding data and social media journalism to their skill set.

What is provided?

  • Equipment
    • Ideally you should have your own laptop, but computers will be provided for those who do not have one.
  • Lunch and refreshments
  • Travel assistance
    • We are based in Claremont, five minute walk from a train station, and we are on both bus and taxi routes. We understand travelling can be a prohibitive expense for some candidates and we will provide transport assistance for those able to motivate it.
