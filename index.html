Winter School

Data and Social Media Journalism Cadet School

Sadly, the media managers and bean counters in South Africa often don’t see the value of data and social media journalism, especially how it could be monetised. As a result they are generally not prepared to invest in growing these skills and resources in newsrooms.

In addition, as few journalism schools offer either of these forms of journalism in their curricula, there is a dearth of these skills in South Africa. The result is that these skills are often scarce and, as a result, prohibitively expensive.

The Winter School aims to fill this gap, teaching working journalists the fundamentals of data and data-driven journalism, as well as social media as a journalism tool, with a focus on discovery and verification of content. Participants will be introduced to all aspects of data and social media journalism, with structured course material. This will be coupled with hands-on practical work to ensure the learnings are fully internalised. The aim is for participants to “hit the ground running” and be able to be able to incorporate their new skills in their day-to-day works as journalists.

What do we cover?

Basic Journalism

Brush up on some of the basics, including generating story ideas, contact and source building, interviewing techniques and ethics.

Data Proficiency

Follow the data pipeline from discovering data sources and scraping and cleaning the data.

Data Viz and Storytelling

Uncover the stories hidden in the data and how best to tell and visualise your story.

The Journalist's Toolkit

Free tools that will help you to be a better journalist.

Social Media Journalism

Social media should be an important part of your work as a journalist. We will focus on the fundamentals, including using social media to find experts and develop sources, as a rich source of stories. There will be a also be a major focus on verification of social media content and tools and protocols.

Who should apply?

Freelance or full-time journalists interested in adding data and social media journalism to their skill set.

What is provided?

  • Equipment
    • Ideally you should have your own laptop, but computers will be provided for those who do have one.
  • Lunch and refreshments
  • Travel assistance
    • We are based in Claremont, close to train stations and taxi routes. We understand travelling can be a prohibitive expense for some candidates and we will provide transport assistance for those able to motivate it.
